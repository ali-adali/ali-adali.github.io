Law of Cosines

The sides of $\triangle ABC$ are $a$, $b$, $c$ and $c$ is seen by $\angle C$. Then $$ c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2\,a\,b\,\cos C . $$

Proof.

  1. Draw the height $AD$. Trigonometry on $\triangle ADC$ yields \begin{align} AD &= b\sin C,\\ CD &= b\cos C, \end{align} and thus $$BD = a-b\cos C.$$
  2. Pythagorean Theorem on $\triangle ABD$ implies \begin{align} c^2 &= (b \sin C)^2 + (a-b\cos C)^2\\ &=b^2\,\sin^2 C + a^2 +b \cos^2 C - 2\,a\,b\cos C\\ &=a^2 + b^2( \sin^2 C + \cos^2 C) - 2\,a\,b\cos C\\ &=a^2 + b^2 - 2\,a\,b\cos C \end{align} as $sin^2 X + \cos^2 X =1$ always. The proof is complete $\square$
