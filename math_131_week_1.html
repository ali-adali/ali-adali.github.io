A set is a collection of objects.


An object in a set is called an element.


We denote a set by listing elements inside braces $\{ \}$.


A set is determined by its elements, and repetitions or rearrangements in a listing do not affect the set.

Example: $\{ 1,2,3,3,3 \}$ and $\{1,3,2\}$ and $\{1,2,3\}$ are the same sets.


Some Important Sets:

$$\text{set of positive integers} = \{1, 2, 3, \cdots \} = \mathbb{N}$$ $$\text{set of integers} = \{\cdots, -3,-2,-1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \cdots \} = \mathbb{Z}$$ $$\text{set of rational numbers} =\left\{ \frac{a}{b} \colon a,b \text{ are integers and }b\neq 0 \right\} =\mathbb{Q}$$


Definition: A number is called irrational if it cannot be written as $\frac{a}{b}$ for some integers $a$ and $b$.


Definition: Combination of rational and irrational numbers constitute the real numbers.

Commutative property (CP)

(You are allowed to switch the order of the numbers in a summation or a product) \begin{equation} \begin{split} a+b &= b+a\\ ab &= ba \end{split} \end{equation}


Associative property (AP)

(You are allowed to change the order of paranthesis ) \begin{equation} \begin{split} a+(b+c) &= (a+b) + c \\ a(bc) &= (ab)c \end{split} \end{equation}


Distribution Property (DP)

(Product distribures over summation.) \begin{equation} a(b+c) = ab + ac \end{equation}


Inverse Properties (IP)

\begin{equation} \begin{split} a + (-a) &= 0\\ a \cdot \frac{1}{a} &= 1 \text{ for }a \neq 0 \end{split} \end{equation}

$-a$ is called the negative of $a$

$\frac{1}{a}$ is called the reciprocal of $a$.

A combination of symbols, numbers and operations is called an algebraic expression


Definition: A polynomial in $x$ is an algebraic expression of the form $$c_nx^n + c_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+c_1x + c_0$$ where $n$ is a nonnegative integer and $c_0,c_1, \cdots, c_n$ are constants.

6. Long Division

A long division has the form \begin{equation} \frac{a\,x^n + \cdots }{ b\,x^m+\cdots } \end{equation} where each term is a polynomial in $x$. We use the following strategy to simplify a long division:

Strategy

(i) if the power of $x$ in the numerator is less than the power of $x$ in the denominator, ( $n>m$ ),then subtract and add $$\frac{ax^n}{bx^m} = \frac{a}{b}x^{n-m}$$ from the expression to obtain \begin{align*} \frac{a\,x^n + \cdots }{ b\,x^m+\cdots }&=\frac{a\,x^n + \cdots }{ b\,x^m+\cdots } - \frac{a}{b}x^{n-m} + \boxed{ \frac{a}{b}x^{n-m} }\\ &=\frac{a\,x^n + \cdots - (a\,x^n +\cdots ) }{ b\,x^m+\cdots } + \boxed{ \frac{a}{b}x^{n-m} }\\ &= \frac{c\,x^{n-1} + \cdots}{ b\,x^m+\cdots } + \boxed{ \frac{a}{b}x^{n-m} } \end{align*} and continue this process until the power of $x$ on the top is less than the power of $x$ on the bottom

(ii) if the power of $x$ in the numerator is less than the power of $x$ in the denominator then stop.
