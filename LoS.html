Law of Sines

In $\triangle ABC$ sides $a$, $b$, $c$ are seen by $\angle A$, $\angle B$ and $\angle C$. Then $$ \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} = 2R, $$ where $R$ is the radius of the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$.

Proof.

  1. Draw diameter $BD$, thus, $$BD = 2R.$$
  2. Draw $DC$. $BD$ is diameter hence, $$\angle DCB = 90^{\circ}.$$
  3. $\angle A$ and $\angle D$ see the same arc $BC$, hence, $$\angle BDC = A.$$
  4. Trigonometry on $\triangle BDC$ yields $$\sin A = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}= \frac{a}{2R}.$$
  5. Hence, $$2R = \frac{a}{\sin A}.$$ Applying similar steps from $B$ and $C$ yield the result: $$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} = 2R,$$ and the proof is complete $\square$
