Generalized Cauchy-Schwartz Inequality

Ali ADALI

14 July, 2021

Abstract

In this brief article, I present a proof of a generalized form of the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. Notably, the argument employs the forward-backward induction technique, originally introduced by Cauchy in his proof of the arithmetic mean-geometric mean inequality. This approach highlights a natural connection between the result and its historical origins.

Theorem 1.

Let $K$ and $n$ be positive integers, let $a_{i,k} \geq 0$ be real numbers for $i=1,\cdots, n$ and $k=1,\cdots, K$. Then \begin{equation} \label{ineq} \prod_{i=1}^{n}\left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{i,k}^{n} \right) \geq \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} \prod_{i=1}^{n}{a_{i,k}} \right)^{n} \end{equation}

Remark 2.

When $n=2$ the inequality is equivalent to Cauchy-Schwartz inequality.

Proof.

It is observed that when $n=2^{N}$, \eqref{ineq} holds after applying the Cauchy-Schwartz inequality for sufficiently many times: \begin{equation} \label{ineq2} \begin{split} \prod_{i=1}^{2^{N}}\left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{i,k}^{2^N} \right) &= \prod_{i=1}^{2^{N-1}}\left[ \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{i,k}^{2^N} \right) \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{i+2^{N-1},k}^{2^N} \right) \right] \\ & \geq \prod_{i=1}^{2^{N-1}}\left[ \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} \left( a_{i,k} a_{i+2^{N-1},k} \right)^{2^{N-1}} \right)^{2} \right]\\ &\geq \prod_{i=1}^{2^{N-2}} \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} \left( a_{i,k} a_{i+2^{N-2},k} a_{i+2\cdot2^{N-2},k} a_{i+3\cdot2^{N-2},k} \right)^{2^{N-2}} \right)^{2^2} \\ & \vdots\\ &\geq \left( \sum_{k=1}^{K}\prod_{i=1}^{2^{N}}{a_{i,k}} \right)^{2^N}. \end{split} \end{equation} Secondly, let $2^N < n < 2^{N+1}$. Applying \eqref{ineq2} with \begin{equation} a_{i,k} \to \left\{\begin{array}{ll} a_{i,k}^{\frac{n}{2^{N+1}}}& \text{if } i\leq n \\ \prod_{i=1}^{n}{a_{i,k}^{\frac{1}{2^{N+1}}} } &\text{if } 2^{N+1} > i > n \end{array}\right. \end{equation} one obtains \begin{equation} \prod_{i=1}^{n}\left( \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{i,k}^{n} \right) \left[ \sum_{k=1}^{K} \prod_{i=1}^{n}{a_{i,k}} \right]^{2^{N+1} - n} \geq \left[ \sum_{k=1}^{K} \prod_{i=1}^{n}{a_{i,k}} \right]^{2^{N+1}} \end{equation} and the result is obtained after cancellation, completing the proof. $\square$
