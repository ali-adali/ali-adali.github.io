Pythagorean Theorem.

Let $a$, $b$, and $c$ be the sides of a right triangle where $c$ is the hypotenuse. Then, $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2.$$

Proof.

  1. Extend $CA$ by $a$ and $CB$ by $b$ units to points $D$ and $E$.
  2. Construct $CEFD$ square.
  3. Mark points $G$ on $DF$ and $H$ on $EF$ such that \begin{align} DG=FH&=b,\\ GF=HE&=a. \end{align} Draw $AG$, $GH$ and $HB$ to form 4 triangles and a quadrilateral.
  4. All triangles have a Side-Angle-Side configuration: $$(a,90^{\circ},b)$$ Hence all triangles are congruent due to SAS congruency.
  5. Congruency implies $$ AB = AG = GH = HB =c, $$ and if $\angle ABC = \theta$, then $$\angle GAD =\theta.$$
  6. Hence, $$\angle CAB = 90-\theta$$ and one inner angle of the quadrilateral is $$\angle BAG = 90.$$ All inner angles of $AGHB$ are $90^{\circ}$ by symmetry. Thus, $AGHB$ is a square with side $c$.
  7. Area(Big Square) = Area(Small Square)+4×Area(Triangle), hence: \begin{align} (a+b)^2 &= c^2 + 4\times \frac{1}{2}\,a\,b,\\ a^2+b^2+2ab &=c^2 + 2ab,\\ a^2+b^2 &= c^2. \end{align} and the result follows. The proof is complete $\square$
